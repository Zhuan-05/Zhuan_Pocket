The meeting focused on the fundamentals of Lean Manufacturing, organized into three modules:
1. The 8 Wastes (TIMWOODS)
2. 5S / 6S Workplace Management 3. Kaizen Improvement Project—full workflow & key operation tips
  
II. The 8 Wastes – TIMWOODS  
A core tool for identifying inefficiency; eight categories:

![[1000067697.heic]]
1. Transportation
2. Inventory
3. Motion
4. Waiting
5. Over-production
6. Over-processing
7. Defects
8. Skills (under-utilized human talent)  
  
III. 5S / 6S Management  
A foundational shop-floor system to standardize and speed up work.  
A. 5S Elements (Romaji & English) 1. Seiri – Sort: remove unnecessary items.
2. Seiton – Set in Order: arrange remaining items for instant access. 3. Seiso – Shine: clean the area and keep it spotless.
4. Seiketsu – Standardize: turn the first 3S into documented standards.
5. Shitsuke – Sustain: audit regularly and maintain discipline.  
B.
6S Addition - Safety: explicit focus on hazard prevention and risk control.

IV. Kaizen Improvement Project  
A. End-to-End Workflow 1. Theme selection – open a Kaizen card in Retros; state the improvement target. 2. Go & See – take an iPad to the shop-floor, capture issues in Redzone (photos / video). 3. Idea generation – return to the classroom, brainstorm solutions as a team. 4. Screening – score each idea on impact, cost, ease; pursue only high-scorers. 5. Action – convert chosen ideas into concrete tasks, each with an owner and due-date.  
B. Follow-up  
Hold regular review meetings, update task status, and keep refining the project.  
C. System Tips for Creating a Kaizen Project 1. Entry point – click the “+” icon on the KPI dashboard. 2. Mandatory fields – title, owner, sponsor, participants, location, objective. 3. Fish-bone diagram – auto-generated, cannot be deleted or renamed; add all causes in one batch.
